Avoid using the “embed” option to insert a social media post into your publication. This can be unstable for preservation and for long-term sustainability since posts or accounts may be deleted. If the social media post is integral to the work, consider first taking a screenshot that can be embedded into the publication as an image. Underneath, a caption should indicate the origin of the post. In addition, use a web archive service such as <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20220201020148/http://archive.is/" target="_blank" data-versionurl="https://web.archive.org/web/20210531102537/https://archive.is/" data-versiondate="2021-05-31">archive.today</a> or <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20220201020148/https://web.archive.org/save/" target="_blank" data-versionurl="https://web.archive.org/web/20210915131158/https://web.archive.org/save/" data-versiondate="2021-09-15">Internet Archive’s Save Page Now service</a> to create a copy of the post—be sure to test the results, since archiving social media posts can be unreliable. The two links (live and archived) could be referenced as a citation or footnote depending on local practices.
